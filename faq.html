4-H is a worldwide youth development program available in every state and over eighty countries. It is open to all youth aged 5 to 19, who want to have fun, learn new skills, and explore the world. In return, youth who participate in 4-H find a supportive environment and opportunities for hands-on or "experiential" learning about things that interest them. They also get what all young people need to succeed in life - the confidence, compassion and connections with caring adults to make contributions to their communities. The mission of Cornell Cooperative Extension 4-H Youth Development is to unleash the power of youth and adults through diverse opportunities that meet community needs.

The Botany Besties are a group of kids ages 8 to 18 that want to learn more in depth about Plant Sciences, do experiments, and, following the mission of 4-H, learn hands-on through activities and field trips. The Botany Besties also work on community service projects, such as our Food Donation Project. As we learn, we hope to make friends, develop life skills, and contribute to our community. If you would like to join us, fill out our Join form. We hope to see you at our next meeting!

4-H Appropriate, sometimes abbreviated as 4-H A, is what is deemed appropriate in the 4-H setting. This generally includes respect, good sportsmanship, supportive actions to one another, and other positive actions. In 4-H, we discourage swearing, immodest dress, and other activities deemed inappropriate. If you have any questions, please fill out the Contact form and one of our co-leaders will follow up.

4-H has many clubs for different areas of interest, such as Animal Science clubs, Art clubs, and Cooking clubs. To see the full list, you can go to the CCE Tompkins website.
